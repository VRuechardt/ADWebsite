Lectures

Each day of the week started off with an inspiring lecture given by an expert in the respective field. While all students possessed basic programming skills, the lectures covered a broad range of related fields necessary to understand the fundamental requirements and challenges of autonomous flying.


Patrick Christ

CDTM Management Team

Introduction

Patrick was the course coordinator and kicked off the project with a lecture on software including an introduction to Python and libardrone.


Dr. Seyed-Ahmad Ahmadi

Chair for Computer Aided Medical Procedures & Augmented Reality, TUM

Computer Vision & Image Processing

In a lecture on computer vision and image processing, Dr. Ahmadi provided the students with insights into how to recognize and track objects like balls or pictures.


Dr. Jürgen Sturm

Metaio GmbH

Robotics

Dr. Sturm talked about his recent research in the field of autonomous drones and introduced the students to the basics of measurement, control and regulation technology.


Laura Bechthold

CDTM Management Team

Ethics

In a thought provoking ethic session the students experienced the up- and downsides of autonomous drones performing a role play.
